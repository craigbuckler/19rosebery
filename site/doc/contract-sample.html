Definitions

The following words defined in quotes ("") have the associated meanings unless inconsistent with the context.

"appropriate": something an average person, having given due thought and consideration, would consider appropriate.

"consent": you must seek our consent in writing to avoid any confusion.

"deposit": any amount paid by you, or by someone on your behalf, to use as security against your obligations under this agreement. Section 7 provides deposit terms.

"either party": the owner or tenant.

"emergency": a risk of damage to the property, neighbouring properties, or life.

"fair wear and tear": natural degradation through normal usage. For example, a carpet may become threadbare. You are not responsible for damaged items when used in a normal way. Breakages are not considered fair wear and tear.

"fixtures and fittings": items listed in the inventory or items replacing them. This includes the fixtures, fittings, appliances, furnishings, floorings, carpets, ceilings, and wall coverings. You are responsible for all fixtures and fittings until the end of the contract even if you choose not to use them.

"insured risk": any circumstance covered by the owner's insurance policy. The owner is not legally obligated to have insurance.

"inventory"/"contents": the document that describes the condition of the property, fixtures, and fittings. Tenants must check and sign the inventory before the tenancy commences.

"joint and several liability": each tenant must comply with the obligations of this agreement individually and together. The owner may seek to claim damages against one or more tenants and/or guarantors irrespective of individual fault.

"nuisance": a non-exhaustive term to cover a broad range of issues that are capable of causing fear, annoyance, or concern to others.

"owner"/"landlord": the current or future legal owner(s) of the property. The owner may appoint an agent to act on their behalf.

"parties": both the owner and tenants.

"property": the address defined the summary to be rented to tenants. This includes the interior, exterior, windows, drains, gutters, pipes, outbuildings, gardens, and garages.

"relevant persons": any other person or company paying the deposit or rental fees on your behalf.

"sublet": allowing people who are not authorized tenants to live at the property.

"tenant"/"occupier": those residing in the property.

"us/we/our": the owner.

"utilities": property services including council tax, water, drainage, electricity, gas, fuel, broadband, television, and parking.

"you/your": the tenant.

References to multiples (e.g. tenants, guarantors, pets) is also a reference to the singular (tenant, guarantor, pet).

References to any legislation includes a reference to amended, replaced, and subordinate legislation made by UK Parliament.

Deposit

You have paid a deposit outlined in the contract summary to act as security for the contract.

The deposit is covered by an appropriate Tenancy Deposit Protection Scheme of the owners choosing. Full details are provided on receipt of the deposit.

The deposit can compensate the owner for any of the following purposes:

  1. Any loss caused by damage to the property when compared to the inventory, except for repairs that are our responsibility to fix;
  2. Any cleaning required to restore the property to its pre-contract condition, as set out in the inventory;
  3. Any removal of tenant items left in the property at the end of the contract;
  4. Any unpaid utilities, bills, or fines you are responsible for paying;
  5. Any unpaid rental fees;
  6. Any sums owed by you to a third party, including agents and contractors;
  7. Any legal costs you are ordered to pay by a court.

The following clauses apply at the end of the tenancy:

  1. The owner tell you within 10 working days after the expiration date they intend to make deductions from the deposit.
  2. If there is no dispute, we will arrange for the deposit to be repaid.
  3. If you intend to dispute any deductions, you must let us know in writing within 10 working days of the proposed deductions. If the parties are unable to resolve the dispute, either may apply to Court for a money judgment/release of the deposit. We will hold the deposit until the Court makes a final decision.

We refund the deposit, minus deductions, to the lead tenant.

Any interest accrued on the deposit belong to the owner or the Tenancy Deposit Protection Scheme.

Tenant agreements

Rental fee

Pay the rental fee.

If rent is more than 14 days overdue, pay interest at 3% above the Bank of England base rate until paid in full. Payments are allocated against the oldest outstanding fee owed to minimise interest.

Not withhold any of the rental fee for any reason including disputes with the owner.

The owner can change rental fees every twelve months by issuing a Section 13 notice.

Use of the property

Pay all utility charges and fines including, but not limited to, council tax, waste disposal, electricity, gas, water, sewage, telephone, broadband, television, and parking.

Behave appropriately in the property and not commit criminal or immoral acts.

Be considerate to neighbours and other passers-by and not to cause nuisance.

Use the property as a private residence. It must not be used for business except for working from home.

Use the property and any fixtures and fittings in an appropriate manner.

Not damage the property, fixtures, or fittings.

Pay for the cost of replacing or repairing removed or damaged items. An allowance is made for the lifespan of the item and fair wear and tear.

Ensure the house is adequately heated during cold periods.

Prevent pest infestation by regularly disposing rubbish and maintaining acceptable levels of hygiene.

Prevent obstructions to drains or gutters as necessary.

Not store combustible, offensive, dangerous fluids, fuels or materials on the property.

If you are vacating the property for a period of time:

  1. Secure the property.
  2. During periods of low temperature, turn off the water stop cock and keep adequate heating on.
  3. Notify the owner if the property is vacant for more than 14 days.

Cleaning

Keep the property interior, exterior, and garden clean and tidy during the contract.

Prevent legionnaires disease:

  1. Retain hot water temperatures of at least 60°C.
  2. Regularly clean and disinfect shower heads.
  3. If the property is vacant for more than seven days, flush all toilets and run all taps and showers for at least two minutes on your return.

Alterations

Do not make alterations or disability adaptions to the property without the owner's consent. The owner will not unreasonably withhold consent, but you must return the property to its original state at the end of the tenancy.

Breakages

Replace lightbulbs, batteries (such as smoke and carbon monoxide alarms), fuses, broken glass, etc. as necessary.

Ventilation

Ensure that the property is adequately ventilated.

Minimize water vapour. Use dehumidifiers, extractor fans, tumble dryers, or washing lines. Do not dry washing inside the property on radiators or heaters.

Sub-letting

You must not sub-let the property.

Pets

You must not keep pets in the property without the owner's consent. The owner may charge additional monthly rental fees for pets.

Smoking

You must not smoke or vape in the property without the owner's consent.

Emergencies

In an emergency, you agree to:

  1. Take appropriate remedial action to prevent further damage to the property.
  2. Report any issue which is the owner's responsibility to repair. We are not responsible for repairs unless you notify us.
  3. Allow us to access the property for inspections and repairs. If access is unreasonably refused, you are liable for costs or losses that result from that refusal.

Access

You agree to:

  1. Provide access to the owner to inspect the property at least twice per year by prior arrangement. Further inspections may be required to check remedial actions.
  2. Forward letters or notices addressed to the owner as soon as possible.
  3. Not change door, window, or other locks and pay reasonable costs for replacing lost keys.
  4. Allow access to any potential property tenants or purchasers. A sign may be placed on or near the grounds if the property is re-let or sold.

Tenant notice period

The tenant has the right to end this agreement by giving at least one months' notice in writing.

End of tenancy

At the end of your tenancy, you agree to return the property in the same condition as you first occupied it, accepting fair wear and tear. This includes:

  1. Cleaning the property to the same standard received on check-in.
  2. Replacing or repairing any lost or damaged items.
  3. Removing all adaptions and personal items from the property.
  4. Notifying all utility and council tax authorities of the date of termination and paying outstanding accounts.
  5. Delivering all keys to the owner.
  6. Providing the owner with contact details and a forwarding address.

Items not removed from the property at the end of the tenancy are deemed to be abandoned. The owner will dispose of these items and charge the tenant associated costs.

Breach of tenancy

The tenant shall pay the owner's reasonable legal costs and expenses incurred by the enforcement or remedy of any breach of this Assured Shorthold Tenancy Agreement.

Guarantor agreements

The guarantor guarantees that the tenant shall pay the guaranteed rent and observe and perform the obligations of this Assured Shorthold Tenancy Agreement.

Guarantors are jointly responsible with tenants to compensate the property owner for any loss, damage, costs, or other expenses arising directly or indirectly from any breach of this Assured Shorthold Tenancy Agreement.

Guarantors agree to pay, cover, and compensate the owner for all losses, claims, liabilities, costs, legal fees, and expenses arising from:

  1. tenants defaulting at any time, or
  2. tenants being declared bankrupt where the bankruptcy trustee elects to disclaim the tenancy.

The guarantor agrees to pay any costs, fees, and expenses of the owner in enforcing this guarantee and the obligations of the tenancy irrespective of court proceedings.

The guarantor is not be released from the liabilities arising under the tenancy or any variation/amendment of the tenancy.

The guarantee applies to this agreement and any renewal, extension, or continuation of the tenancy.

The guarantee is binding. It cannot be revoked and shall continue beyond the guarantor's death or bankruptcy throughout the tenancy period.

Owner agreements

The owner will ensure the property and contents outlined in the inventory abide with all safety regulations. Tenants are provided with the latest safety inspection certificates.

The tenant may quietly enjoy the property without any unlawful interruption by the owner or any person working on the owner's behalf.

Maintenance

The tenant must report damages to the owner as soon as possible.

For cases of fair wear and tear, the owner is responsible for maintaining:

  1. The structure and exterior of the property including drains, gutters, and external pipes.
  2. Installations for the supply of water, gas, and electricity. This does not include fixtures, fittings, or appliances that use those utilities.
  3. Sanitary appliances including sinks, baths, and showers.
  4. Installations for heating and hot water, such as boilers.
  5. Appliances provided in the inventory, such as ovens.

Except in emergencies, if you pay for repairs that are the owner's responsibility, we will not repay your costs unless you give us reasonable opportunity to make those repairs.

We have no legal obligation to improve the property, although we may choose to do so.

Uninhabitable property

The owner is obligated to ensure the property is fit for human habitation. In the event the property becomes uninhabitable by an insured risk:

  • we will provide alternative accommodation and the rental continues to be payable, OR
  • you can find alternative accommodation and the rental ceases to be payable;

If the property is not habitable within one week, either party has the right to bring the contract to an immediate end.

We are not be responsible for your losses in the above situations.

Forfeiture

We may forfeit this agreement and take steps to recover possession of the property if:

  1. any part of the rental fee is owed more than 14 days after becoming payable, or
  2. you have breached the terms of this agreement, or
  3. you become bankrupt or entered into a voluntary arrangement with your creditors.

Notices

The address at which you may send notices (including notices in proceedings) to us is shown in the summary.

Notices may be served on you by recorded or registered delivery, first class post, hand delivery, or email.

  1. For notices sent by post, we will assume delivery within two working days.
  2. For notices hand delivered or emailed before 16:30 on a working day, it is considered as received that day.

Owner notice period

The owner has the right to end your tenancy using a Section 8 notice which provides the tenant with at least two months notice. Two weeks notice can be given when the tenant breaches the terms of this agreement, e.g. failure to pay rent.
